Undergraduate Thesis

Augmented Reality Visualization System of
Polycystic Kidney Disease for Preoperative Planning
2019

  • View on GitHub

Thesis Abstract

This thesis presents a partial solution of a wearable augmented reality (AR) system that reconstructs the anatomy of a patient’s kidneys in an interactive three-dimensional (3D) model based on preoperative CTs scanned for improving the current treatment planning system of laparoscopic cyst decortication. This research provides a pathway to a more accurate treatment planning system utilizing wearable AR technology and describes how an AR 3D model with a high degree of detail can provide more information to medical professionals when doing procedural planning. The use of wearable augmented reality with medical interventions improves efficiency and effectiveness, allowing for 2D medical images to be reconstructed in 3D and spatial information. Having this incorporation between wearable AR and medical procedures will allow for more accurate and effective treatment planning. Further development will allow medical professionals to collaboratively interact with the 3D model in a 3D interactive environment real-time, resulting in a potential increase of quality in treatment planning. Enabling medical professionals to collaborate, view and interact with the same patient data is a unique feature of the Microsoft HoloLens which is the wearable device used in this application.

The Problem

Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD) is a life-threatening incurable genetic disorder resulting in the formation and growth of multiple fluid-filled cysts on the kidneys. PKD is the fourth leading cause of kidyney failures and occurs about 1 in every 500 births in Canada, affecting 12.5 million worldwide. Despite PKD affecting so many, it remains that PKD receives very little funding or media attention.

Currently, the only way to diagnose for PKD is through image scansfor cysts that may have grown on the kidneys once patients have begun to experience symptoms relating to the possibilities of cysts. Unfortunately, there are no 'treatments' for PKD, but rather 'ways to manage' it. In some cases, if the cysts on the kidneys grow to be intrusive and cause discomfort/pain; patients may undergo Laparoscopic Cyst Decortication (LCD).

However, in treatments like LCD which is a minimally invasive procedure, surgeons will operate with limited field of views as surgeons must refer to laparoscopic video, intraoperative ultrasounds or preoperative image scans. Particularly, the problem with laparoscopic video and intraoperative ultrasounds is the reduced spatial information; while preoperative image scans are two-dimensional, reducing the level of detail and depth to the anatomy of the patients’ kidneys.
